Choose the Right Business Structure

Business Name (Enterprise/Sole Proprietorship)

  • Simple and low-cost registration via CAC.
  • No separate legal entity—owner personally liable for debts.
  • Profits taxed as personal income (PIT): 0% on first ₦800,000.
  • Best for micro/small solo operations.

Limited Liability Company (Ltd/Private Limited)

  • Separate legal entity—limited personal liability.
  • Small companies (turnover ≤₦100m + assets ≤₦250m) often 0% CIT & exempt from Development Levy.
  • Easier access to funding, contracts, and credibility.
  • Recommended for growth-oriented businesses.

Many alumni start with Business Name and upgrade to Ltd as revenue grows—enjoying tax savings and protection.

How to Register Your Business (CAC 2026 Process)

  1. Name Search & Reservation — Check availability on CAC portal; reserve name (₦500 fee).
  2. Prepare Documents — ID, passport photos, Memorandum & Articles of Association (for Ltd).
  3. Online Registration — Submit via cac.gov.ng; fees from ₦10,000 (Business Name) to ₦50,000+ (Ltd).
  4. Pay Stamp Duties & Fees — Electronic payment integrated.
  5. Obtain Certificate — Digital certificate issued within days.
  6. Post-Registration — Get TIN from NRS, open corporate bank account, register for VAT if needed.

Key Compliance Tips (2026 Tax Reforms)

  • Register for TIN immediately after CAC—mandatory for all transactions.
  • File annual returns on time (companies: March 31; individuals: June 30).
  • Track expenses for deductions; use accounting software.
  • If turnover >₦100m, register for VAT and file monthly.
  • Leverage small company exemptions if eligible—huge savings.
  • Implement e-invoicing for VAT compliance.
  • Keep records for 6 years; prepare for potential audits.
  • Consult professionals for complex structures or incentives.
