Endowment Committee

Securing Sustainability. Preserving Legacy.

A long-term financial vision designed to protect the future of our association beyond individual tenures and generations.

An endowment is a permanent financial asset established to support an institution’s mission over the long term. Unlike regular donations that are spent immediately, endowment funds are invested, with only a portion of the returns used to support agreed priorities.

For an alumni association such as ours, an endowment represents stability, foresight, and a collective commitment to impact that outlives individual leadership cycles.

Why an Endowment Matters

A disciplined approach to financial continuity and institutional strength.

Financial Continuity

An endowment ensures the association can fund strategic initiatives consistently, regardless of annual fundraising fluctuations or economic conditions.

Generational Impact

Contributions made today continue working decades into the future, supporting students, infrastructure, and alumni programs yet to come.

Strong Governance

Endowments operate under clear governance, accountability, and oversight, reinforcing trust among members, donors, and stakeholders.

Strategic Independence

A well-managed endowment allows the association to plan proactively, respond decisively to opportunities, and reduce reliance on emergency funding.

Committee Status

The Endowment Committee has not yet been constituted. When established, it will be responsible for defining governance structures, investment policies, risk controls, and transparency standards for the endowment fund.

Committee members will be selected based on integrity, financial competence, and a proven commitment to safeguarding the long-term interests of the association.
